BULK

Why BULK Exists

DeFi has a fundamental performance problem. While centralized exchanges can process thousands of trades per second with millisecond response times, decentralized exchanges are orders of magnitude slower. This isn't just an inconvenience—it's a barrier that keeps professional traders and institutional capital out of DeFi.

The Speed Problem

Traditional DEXs process every single order on the blockchain. This means:

High latency: Each trade takes seconds to confirm instead of milliseconds. In fast-moving markets, this delay can mean the difference between profit and loss.

Limited throughput: Even Solana, the fastest blockchain, can only handle so many transactions per second. During high volume, orders get delayed or fail entirely.

Expensive execution: Every order placement, modification, or cancellation costs gas fees. For high-frequency strategies, these costs add up quickly.

The Custody Problem

Centralized exchanges solve the performance problem but create a new one: you don't control your funds. Every major centralized exchange has been hacked, frozen accounts, or gone bankrupt taking user funds with them.

BULK solves this by keeping execution speed off-chain while keeping settlement on-chain. Your funds stay in your wallet. Trades are proven cryptographically by validator consensus. The blockchain verifies everything happened correctly.

The Core Insight

Not everything needs to happen on the blockchain immediately. Order matching can happen at memory speed off-chain, as long as the results are provably correct and eventually settled on-chain. This is what BULK does: it separates the time-sensitive work (matching) from the security-critical work (settlement), optimizing each for what it does best.
